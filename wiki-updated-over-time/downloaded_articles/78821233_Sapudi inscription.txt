The '''Sapudi inscription''' ({{langx|jv|ꦥꦿꦱꦱ꧀ꦠꦶꦤꦸꦱꦱꦥꦸꦣꦪ꧀|prasasti nusa Sapuday}}; {{langx|mad|prasastè nosa Sapoday}}), or '''Koattas inscription''' ({{langx|id|prasasti dusun Koattas}}) is an [[Javanese language|Eastern Javanese]] inscription discovered at the Koattas urban area of Gayam district in the [[Indonesia]]n region of [[Sapudi Islands|Sapudi]] in [[East Java]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Prasasti Dusun Koattas |language=id |trans-title= Koattas Inscription |url=https://www.lontarmadura.com/sejarah-peninggalan-budaya-pulau-sepudi/prasasti-dusun-koattas/ |publisher= Lontar Madura|year=2012}}</ref>

These stone inscription was found in 1988 by a laborer during the excavation process of the ''[[Masjid]]'' (a Muslim prayer place) renovation,<ref name="LM">{{cite web |title= Menengok Kediaman dan Asta Panembahan Wirokromo |language= id |trans-title=Glance at Panembahan Wirakrama’s Historical House and Burial |url= https://www.lontarmadura.com/menengok-kediaman-asta-panem-wirokromo/2/ |publisher= Lontar Madura |year=2012}}</ref> located within the historical Asta Blingi complex.
The inscription written in [[Madurese language|Madurese]]-type of Eastern Javanese (most of it appears to be the [[Old Javanese]]), using [[Pegon script|Arabic–Javanese]] writing system (known locally in Sapudi as {{lang|mad|Pèghu}}, derived from Javanese {{lang|jv|Pegon}}), with several mentions of Javanese honorifics and some [[Arabic]]-derived words.

Since 2020, according to the Indonesian law on Cultural Preservation, the Asta Blingi complex — location where these inscription located at, is officially determined as Cultural Site, and any historical matters within the region is protected under the law.<ref>{{cite web |title=Resmi, Asta Blingi Sapudi Jadi Situs Cagar Budaya Sumenep |language=id |trans-title=Its Official, Asta Blingi in Sapudi Become the Cultural Site of Sumenep |url= https://jatim.indonesiasatu.co.id/resmi-asta-blingi-sapudi-jadi-situs-cagar-budaya-sumenep|publisher= |year=2021}}</ref>

==Description==
The inscription is made out of [[limestone]] with the size of 34.5 cm × 49.5 cm × 8 cm (13.6 in × 19.5 in × 3.1 in).<ref name="LM"/> A commom material found on the island.

It records the genealogy of several [[Javanese people|Javanese]]-origin monarch leaders of the archipelagic region off the coastal Eastern Java, namely the [[Madura Island|Madura]], [[Raas Island|Raas]], [[Sapudi Island|Sapudi]], [[Kangean Archipelago|Kangean]], as well as [[Makassar]] in [[Southern Sulawesi]].<ref name="LM"/>
